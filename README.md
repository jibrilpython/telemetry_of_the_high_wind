# Telemetry of the High Wind

Telemetry of the High Wind is a digital catalog for preserving the history of atmospheric exploration. Designed for collectors, researchers, and weather instrumentation enthusiasts, the application brings together the scientific tools that once revealed the mysteries of the upper atmosphere—from the earliest weather balloon payloads to the analog instruments that measured the world above the clouds.

Discover and organize a collection of historic meteorological and aeronautical artifacts, including mechanical radiosondes, pressure and temperature sensors, high-altitude radio transmitters, and analog tracking equipment. Whether crafted from metal, glass, or early electrical components, each instrument represents a milestone in the evolution of atmospheric science.

Document every piece in your collection by recording its manufacturer, era, specifications, instrument category, and historical background. Preserve not only the artifact itself, but also the story of how it contributed to humanity's understanding of the skies.

## Bespoke Object Fields (Model "Atmospheric Payload"):

•	Sonde Tracking Index: A unique, system-generated aerospace logging sequence (e.g., THW-SONDE-1102-MET-A).

•	Payload Classification: The functional category of the weather apparatus (e.g., Barosonde, Chronometric Radiosonde, Audio-Modulated Sonde, Radar Target Reflector, Transosonde System).

•	Artisan Hallmark: Fictionalized aerospace laboratories and radio science groups (e.g., Aetheric Sonde Labs, Meridian Aero-Instrument, Sovereign Telemetry Corp).

•	Transmission Frequency Band: The specific radio signal channel used to beam climate data back down to earth, written in Megahertz (e.g., 72.2 MHz, 403 MHz, 1680 MHz).

•	Barometric Sensor Profile: The design of the pressure-responsive cell assembly (e.g., Aneroid capsule stack, bimetallic contract arm, mercury column switch).

•	Battery Chemistry Compound: The legacy power storage type built to survive freezing sub-zero conditions (e.g., Water-activated cuprous chloride cell, heavy zinc-carbon pack, dry silver-zinc block).

•	Enclosure Composition Material: The protective insulation used to house the internal electronics (e.g., Waxed corrugated cardboard, molded balsa wood, early sheet polystyrene foam, tinned steel sheet).

•	Physical Proportions: The total spatial volume of the housing cube, along with total mass including the battery array.

•	Preservation Soundness: The physical state of the recovered package (e.g., Stratospheric moisture corrosion, impact distortion, complete internal wiring continuity).

•	Stratospheric Ground Zero: The fictionalized regional airport launch station, arctic research outpost, or university physics field terrain where the balloon took flight (e.g., Forgotten arctic research camp, historic coastal naval station, mid-continent tracking field).
